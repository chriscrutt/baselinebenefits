Fair questions

Can I use it today?

Not yet. We're building now, and the waitlist gets first access. You'll get one email when it's ready.

What will it cost me?

Nothing. We earn a small cut from pharmacies and stores when we save you money, never from you.

What happens to my data?

Today we store your email and use it to tell you when the app is ready. Once you connect accounts, your data is used to find your savings. We never sell it.

Is this advice?

No. Baseline Benefits helps you use the benefits you already have. We don't give medical, tax, or legal advice, and we never suggest changing your medication.

Baseline Benefits helps you understand and use your health benefits. It's not tax, legal, or medical advice. HSA eligibility depends on your plan and IRS rules, and prices are estimates. We never tell you to change your medication.

  1. Many everyday items are HSA/FSA-eligible, including over-the-counter drugs like aspirin (no prescription needed since the 2020 CARES Act) and sunscreen (SPF 15+, broad-spectrum). Out-of-network care can qualify too: HSA rules follow the IRS definition of qualified medical expenses, not your insurer's network. See IRS Publication 502 and Fidelity's eligible-expenses list. Eligibility varies by plan.
  2. Paying a discounted cash price (for example, with GoodRx) generally does not count toward your insurance deductible, while an insurance copay does. See GoodRx Support.
